# v1.0 — May 2026

## Priority: Find These 5 First

One vendor per category. Handyman is highest priority — broadest coverage across Covered and Complete.

| Priority | Category | Why First |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Handyman | Powers seasonal walkthrough (Covered) + quarterly visit (Complete). Broadest dispatch volume. |
| 2 | HVAC | Highest member anxiety service. Fall/spring tune-ups are the anchor of Covered. |
| 3 | Plumbing | Water heater flush is high-value, low-complexity. Easy first dispatch. |
| 4 | Seasonal/Exterior | Gutter cleaning 2x/year. Batches well. Low barrier to entry. |
| 5 | Electrical | Safety inspection is included annually. Lower volume but high trust value. |

## Where to Find Candidates

### Tier 1 — Warm (start here)
- **Your existing OAKRAA/TTPC trade contacts** — anyone who has worked for you or been recommended to you personally
- **Members and clients who trust you** — "Who do you use for [HVAC/plumbing/handyman]?"
- **Nextdoor and local Facebook groups** — search Aurora neighborhood groups for recommended contractors. Look for names that appear multiple times with positive mentions.

### Tier 2 — Semi-warm
- **Google reviews** — search "HVAC Aurora CO" or "handyman Aurora CO", filter by 4.5+ stars, 20+ reviews. Read reviews for tone — vendors who get mentioned by name ("asked for John specifically") are gold.
- **Angi / HomeAdvisor Aurora listings** — these vendors are already used to platform relationships. Filter for: 4.5+ stars, 50+ reviews, actively accepting new clients.
- **Home Depot Pro desk (Aurora/Centennial stores)** — contractors shop here daily. Introduce yourself, leave a one-pager. The Pro desk staff know the regulars.

### Tier 3 — Cold but targeted
- **Local BNI chapters** — Aurora, Centennial, Parker chapters meet weekly. Trades show up specifically for referral relationships.
- **Aurora Chamber of Commerce member directory** — filter for home services category
- **Colorado Contractors Association / local HVAC association members**

---

## What to Look For — Green Flags

- Solo operator or small team (2-4 people) — right size for early volume
- 3+ years in business — established enough to be reliable
- Strong Google reviews mentioning reliability, communication, and older adult clients
- Responsive — text them at 9am on a weekday. If they reply within 2 hours, that's your person.
- Willing to talk business, not just talk jobs — someone who understands recurring revenue
- Licensed + insured already (confirms professionalism)

## Red Flags

- Immediately asks "how many jobs per month guaranteed?" before hearing the pitch
- Haggles on the rate card before understanding the model
- No online presence / can't be verified
- Dismissive of older adult clients or seems transactional
- Can't provide insurance certificate quickly

## Outreach Sequence

### Step 1: Initial contact (text or call — not email first)
Trades respond to texts faster than emails. Keep it brief.

**Text script:**
> "Hi [Name], my name is Tye — I run a home care platform serving homeowners 55+ in Aurora. I'm building a small network of trusted local [HVAC/handyman/plumbing] vendors and [your name/mutual contact] mentioned you do great work. Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call this week? No pressure — just want to explain what we do and see if there's a fit."

### Step 2: 20-minute intro call
Goal: explain the model, answer questions, qualify fit. NOT to close.

**Call outline:**
1. (2 min) What NoWorry Home does — platform for 55+ homeowners, coordinating home maintenance and family planning
2. (3 min) What we send vendors — guaranteed recurring work, no marketing, predictable payment
3. (5 min) What the seasonal walkthrough looks like — walk through the visit structure, findings logging, simple tasks
4. (5 min) The rate card and loyalty rate — be direct about rates, explain the CAC trade
5. (5 min) Their questions — be honest about current volume (zero/early stage), explain the loyalty rate opportunity

**Key line for the volume question:**
> "I'll be straight with you — we're early stage. Our first members are signing up now. What I can offer you is the loyalty rate, guaranteed payment, and first-mover advantage in your category in this territory. The volume grows as our member base grows. I'm looking for someone who sees the long-term opportunity, not just the next job."

### Step 3: Send the one-pager + rate card
After the call — same day. Email is fine now that rapport is established.

**Email script:**

Subject: NoWorry Home — Vendor Partner Materials

> Hi [Name],
>
> Great talking with you today. As promised, here are the two documents I mentioned:
>
> 1. Vendor Partner Overview — what the program looks like and what we're asking of you
> 2. Vendor Flat Rate Card — the exact rates for every dispatched service
>
> Take a look and let me know if you have any questions. If it feels like a fit, the next step is a short credential review (license + insurance certificate) and a background check for each technician — that's the $49 network activation fee I mentioned.
>
> No pressure on timing. I'd rather have the right vendor than a fast one.
>
> — Tye
> NoWorry Home by OAKRAA
> vendors@noworry-home.com

### Step 4: Follow up once (5-7 days later)
One follow-up only. If no response after that, move to the next candidate.

**Follow-up text:**
> "Hey [Name] — just checking in on the NoWorry Home materials I sent over. Happy to answer any questions. Let me know either way — no hard feelings if the timing isn't right."

## Notes on the Conversation

**On the rate cut question:**
"Our rates are below your retail, yes. But think about what you're not spending: no Google Ads, no Angi leads, no time on estimates that don't convert. We eliminate your customer acquisition cost entirely. The members we send you are scheduled, pre-approved, and pay through us — you never chase an invoice. For the right operator, that trade is worth more than the rate difference."

**On the volume question:**
"We're in launch mode. I'm not going to promise you 50 jobs a month tomorrow. What I can promise is: every job you do well on this platform builds your reputation with members who talk to their adult kids, their neighbors, and their friends. The referral chain from one happy 70-year-old homeowner is worth more than the rate difference over time."

**On the findings logging:**
"This is the part that makes us different from every other service marketplace. After every seasonal walkthrough, you log what you saw — loose railing, aging water heater, cracked caulk. That becomes a dispatch recommendation, and you get that job at full flat rate. You're not just doing a checklist visit — you're building a pipeline for your own follow-on work."
